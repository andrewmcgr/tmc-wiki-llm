## Overview

Without a system, I was shopping 3-4 times a week and throwing away produce that went bad. This workflow gets it down to one trip and near-zero waste.

---

## The Process

1. **Sunday morning: Check what's left** - Open the fridge, note what needs to be used up this week.
2. **Pick 5 dinners** - Choose based on what you have, what's in season, and energy levels (2 easy, 2 medium, 1 ambitious).
3. **Generate the grocery list** - Cross-reference recipes against pantry. Group by store section.
4. **Shop once** - Sunday afternoon. Stick to the list.
5. **Prep what you can** - Wash and chop vegetables, marinate proteins, cook grains in bulk.
6. **Weeknight execution** - Follow the plan. Leftovers become tomorrow's lunch.

---

## What I've Learned

- Planning 7 dinners leads to waste. 5 is the sweet spot (leftovers cover the other 2).
- Always have a "pantry meal" backup (pasta + canned tomatoes + whatever).
- Seasonal produce is cheaper and tastes better. Check what's in season before planning.
- Batch-cooking grains on Sunday saves 20 minutes every weeknight.
